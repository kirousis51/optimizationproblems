# Classical zero-free region constant

## Description of constant

$C_7$ is the least constant such that there are no zeroes $\sigma+it$ of the Riemann zeta function with $|t| \geq 2$ and $\sigma > 1 - \frac{1}{C_7 \log |t|}$.

# The irrationality measure of $\pi$

## Description of constant

We define $C_7$ to be the irrationality measure of $\pi$:

$C_7 := \sup_{\mu\in\mathbb{R}} \mu$ such that $|\pi - p/q| < q^{-\mu}$ for infinitely many rationals $p/q$.

Equivalently, $C_{7}$ is the infimum of all $\nu$ such that for every $\varepsilon>0$ there exists $q_0(\varepsilon)$ with

$$|\pi-\frac{p}{q}| > \frac{1}{q^{\nu+\varepsilon}}$$

for all integers $p$ and all integers $q \ge q_0(\varepsilon)$.

## Additional comments and links

- It is widely conjectured that $C_{7}=2$ (as holds for Lebesgue-a.e. real number), but no improvement over the universal lower bound $2$ is currently known.
- Many upper bounds come from constructing explicit rational approximations to $\pi$ (often via special integrals or hypergeometric constructions) and then converting these to irrationality-measure estimates; see [B2000] for an accessible overview up to Hata's work.
- [Wikipedia page on irrationality measure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Irrationality_measure)
- [Wikipedia page on Dirichlet's approximation theorem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dirichlet%27s_approximation_theorem)
