# Dirichlet divisor problem exponent

## Description of constant

Let $d(n)$ be the divisor function. The Dirichlet divisor problem concerns the error term
$$
\Delta(x)\ :=\ \sum_{n\le x} d(n)\ -\ x(\log x + 2\gamma -1),
$$
where $\gamma$ is Euler's constant.
<a href="#Tsa2010-def-Delta">[Tsa2010-def-Delta]</a>

Define the divisor-problem exponent
$$
\alpha\ :=\ \inf\Bigl\{a\ge 0:\ \Delta(x)=O(x^{a+\varepsilon})\ \text{for all }\varepsilon>0\Bigr\}.
$$
<a href="#Tsa2010-def-alpha">[Tsa2010-def-alpha]</a>

We define
$$
C_{63}\ :=\ \alpha,
$$
the Dirichlet divisor problem exponent.

It is conjectured that $\alpha=1/4$.
<a href="#Tsa2010-conj-1-4">[Tsa2010-conj-1-4]</a>
The best known upper bound is due to Huxley,
$$
\alpha\ \le\ \frac{131}{416}.
$$
<a href="#Tsa2010-ub-131-416">[Tsa2010-ub-131-416]</a>

Hardy's omega result implies that $\Delta(x)=\Omega(x^{1/4})$ (up to logarithmic factors), hence $\alpha\ge 1/4$.
<a href="#Tsa2010-omega-1-4">[Tsa2010-omega-1-4]</a>

The best established range currently is
$$
\frac14\ \le\ C_{63}\ \le\ \frac{131}{416}.
$$
