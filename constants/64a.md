# Gauss circle problem exponent

## Description of constant

Let
$$
N(t)\ :=\ \#\{(m,n)\in\mathbb{Z}^2:\ m^2+n^2\le t^2\}
$$
be the number of integer lattice points inside the (closed) disk of radius $t$ centered at the origin. The Gauss circle problem is to find the smallest exponent $\theta$ such that, for every $\varepsilon>0$,
$$
N(t)\ =\ \pi t^2\ +\ O(t^{\theta+\varepsilon}).
$$
<a href="#CRM2023-def-theta">[CRM2023-def-theta]</a>

We define
$$
C_{64}\ :=\ \theta,
$$
the Gauss circle problem exponent.

The best established upper bound is due to Huxley:
$$
\theta\ \le\ \frac{131}{208}.
$$
<a href="#CRM2023-ub-131-208">[CRM2023-ub-131-208]</a>

Hardy conjectured the optimal exponent $\theta=1/2$.
<a href="#CRM2023-conj-1-2">[CRM2023-conj-1-2]</a>

The best established range currently is
$$
0\ \le\ C_{64}\ \le\ \frac{131}{208}.
$$
